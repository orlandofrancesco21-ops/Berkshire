## Primary valuation framework

Per the industry framework, mature, profitable creative software trades on forward P/E with FCF yield as the income-style cross-check and rule-of-40 as the catch-all. For Adobe specifically:

- **Forward P/E (FY27-FY28)** is the primary lens because Adobe is mature, profitable, and capital-light enough that EBITDA and FCF closely track non-GAAP net income.
- **FCF yield** is the cleanest income-style sanity check at current levels — Adobe's ~9% FCF yield is one of the most extreme in mega-cap software history.
- **EV/FCF** is the slightly cleaner version of FCF yield because it accounts for net cash on balance sheet (~$3B).
- **Rule of 40** (revenue growth + non-GAAP op margin) is the catch-all: Adobe is ~57 (11% growth + 46% margin), which is exceptional and which the current ~10-11x multiple does not respect.
- Generic EV/EBITDA is a useful cross-check but not primary.

**Read on current state:** Adobe is the cheapest it has been since 2008-2009 on every multiple-based metric. The setup is unusual: the operational quality (rule of 40 of 57, NDR ~110%+, FCF margin 40%+) is among the best in software, while the multiple is among the lowest. This is either a structural break being correctly priced or a generational mispricing. The cross-check that's hardest to ignore: a 9% FCF yield on a software franchise growing 11% with 40%+ FCF margins is the kind of yield typically associated with melting-ice-cube businesses (legacy media, declining industrials), not category-leader software.

## Fair value range

The valuation hinges on FY28 (fiscal year ending Nov 2028) earnings power and the exit multiple. Three scenarios:

| Scenario | FY28 EPS (non-GAAP) | Multiple | Fair value | Implied probability |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| **Bear:** AI structurally disrupts; revenue plateau then decline; margin compression to 38%; multiple stays compressed | $19 | 9x (further compression) | **$170** | ~25% |
| **Base:** AI monetization compounds; revenue grows 9-11%; margin stable at 45-47%; multiple recovers modestly | $26 | 16x | **$416** | ~50% |
| **Bull:** Adobe wins the AI workflow battle; AI ARR scales to $2B+; revenue accelerates back toward 13-14%; margin expands to 48%+; multiple re-rates closer to historical | $30 | 22x | **$660** | ~25% |

**Fair value range:** **$170 (bear) — $416 (base) — $660 (bull)**

**Probability-weighted fair value:** ~$415 (0.25 × $170 + 0.50 × $416 + 0.25 × $660)

**Current price vs range:** $240 is ~42% below probability-weighted FV and ~41% above the bear-case fair value. Asymmetry: ~73% upside to base / 175% upside to bull / ~29% downside to bear.

## Sensitivity

The variables that drive the spread:

| Variable | Bear | Base | Bull | FV impact |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| FY28 revenue ($B) | $24 (flat) | $30 | $33 | ~$50-70 per $2B revenue |
| FY28 non-GAAP op margin | 38% | 46% | 49% | ~$30-40 per 200 bps |
| FY28 share count (M) | 390 | 365 | 350 | ~$15-25 per 10M shares (driven by buyback execution) |
| Exit P/E multiple | 9x | 16x | 22x | ~$30 per 2 P/E turns at base case |
| AI ARR FY28 ($B) | $0.3 (stalls) | $1.5 | $3.0 | implicit in revenue and margin |

**The two variables that dominate:** revenue trajectory (does growth hold at 10-12% or decelerate to <8%?) and the exit multiple (does the market re-rate from 10x to 16x as AI fears recede, or does it stay compressed?). The two are correlated — if revenue holds, multiple expands; if revenue rolls, multiple compresses further. The narrative-fundamentals feedback loop is the single biggest source of variance in the FV outcome.

## Cross-checks

Other lenses that should agree:

- **FCF yield approach.** Base-case FY28 FCF: ~$11B on $30B revenue at ~37% FCF/sales. At a 4% FCF yield (still cheap by historical software standards, but a meaningful re-rate from current 9%), market cap = ~$275B = ~$750/share on current share count, or ~$830/share if share count shrinks to 330M via buybacks. **This is well above the P/E base case of $416**, suggesting the FCF lens is more generous. Reconciliation: the gap is the implied multiple — 16x P/E and 4% FCF yield are not consistent at Adobe's current FCF-to-net-income conversion. The P/E-based base case is the conservative cross-check.
- **EV/EBITDA mid-cycle.** Base-case FY28 EBITDA: ~$14B. At 16x EV/EBITDA (mid-cycle), EV = $224B, plus $3B net cash = $227B market cap = ~$620/share. Higher than P/E base case but lower than FCF base case. Roughly checks out — the average of all three cross-checks centres in the $400-600 range.
- **Buyback-adjusted EPS approach.** With $25B of buybacks at $300 average over 3 years, share count shrinks to ~320M by FY28. At FY28 non-GAAP net income of $9.5B (= 46% × $30B × 70% conversion to net income), EPS = $30 on the lower share count. At 14x, FV = $420. Roughly checks out with base case.
- **Sum-of-parts.** Digital Media (~75% of revenue) at 15x peer-comparable EV/EBITDA on $11B EBITDA = $165B. Digital Experience at 12x on $3B EBITDA = $36B. Total EV = $201B + net cash $3B = $204B market cap = ~$555/share. Above the P/E base case — suggests the consolidated P/E might be understating intrinsic value because the high-margin Digital Media business deserves a higher multiple than the consolidated company gets.

**The cross-checks broadly agree that fair value is materially above current $240, centred in the $400-550 range under base assumptions. Bear case is $170, requiring genuine structural decline that hasn't shown in data through Q1 FY26.**
