## What's normal vs what's stretched

**Stretched on the cheap side (extreme):**
- **Adobe at 10-11x forward P/E** is the most extreme valuation outlier in mega-cap quality software in this cycle. The franchise still grows 11%+ at 46% non-GAAP margins. Either the franchise is broken and the multiple is fair, or the market is wrong and this is generational.
- **Autodesk at ~22x forward P/E** is below its 5-year average (~28x). Less extreme than Adobe but also cheap given franchise quality.

**Stretched on the rich side:**
- **Figma at premium multiples** (still finding its public-market valuation post-IPO) trades on growth durability that's unproven through a recession.
- **AI-pure-play public names** (where they exist) trade at high EV/Sales on unproven monetization.

**Normal:**
- **Mid-cycle SaaS** (~22x forward P/E sector median) is roughly mid-range historically. The overall sector isn't expensive — Adobe is anomalously cheap within an unremarkable sector backdrop.

**The big call:** Adobe at 10-11x forward P/E is either a value trap (AI disruption is real and structural) or a 50%+ upside opportunity (multiple compresses on fear that doesn't fully materialise). The probability-weighted answer is the deep-dive question. Autodesk is a less-extreme version of the same question.
