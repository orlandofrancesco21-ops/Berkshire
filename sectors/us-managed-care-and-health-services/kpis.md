## What's normal vs what's stretched

**Stretched on the cheap side:**
- **UNH on normalized 2028 EPS (~$25-28) at $393:** 14-16x forward two-year — below 15-year average. But the optical 22x forward 2026 is on artificially-depressed numerator.
- **CVS at high dividend yield (~5%):** reflects significant balance sheet stress and execution doubt rather than a value setup.
- **Humana** trades cheaply on TTM but has the highest concentration of MA exposure during the rate-cycle reset.

**Stretched on the rich side:**
- **HCA and other provider-side names** have re-rated up on payer-provider rate-shift dynamics. They are the beneficiaries of MA rate cuts that compress payer margins.

**Normal:**
- **Elevance Health** trades at ~13x forward 2027 EPS — roughly the sector median. Less MA exposure, less services exposure, less drama.
- **Cigna** at ~12-14x forward 2027 EPS post-MA-divestiture — clean commercial + PBM exposure.

**The big call:** the sector overall is below historical median multiples, but the relevant comparison is normalized EPS (2028) vs current price. On that basis, UNH and ELV both screen attractive on multiple if you assume MA economics normalize. The cleanest value setup at current pricing is probably ELV (less drama, less regulatory exposure, similar discount to history). UNH offers a wider asymmetry but with materially higher single-stock risk from DOJ and operational reset execution. Berkshire's recent round trip on UNH at near-current prices is the relevant pricing signal.
