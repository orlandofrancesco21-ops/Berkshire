# US managed care & health services — players

May 2026 market caps approximate.

## Tier 1 — must understand

The names that define the sector.

| Ticker | Company | Market cap | Positioning | Why it matters |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| UNH | UnitedHealth Group | ~$360B | Largest US payer (~50M members); deepest vertical integration via Optum ($257B revenue) | Bellwether. Mid-reset under multiple DOJ investigations. Berkshire bought-and-sold within 9 months. The single most consequential setup in the sector. |
| ELV | Elevance Health | ~$95B | #2 by revenue (~46M members); Blues affiliations; Carelon services build | Less MA-exposed, less services-exposed, less drama than UNH. Cleanest "quality at a discount" play. |
| CI | Cigna Group | ~$80B | Commercial + PBM (Evernorth). MA divested 2024. | Cleanest commercial + PBM pure play post-MA divestiture. |
| CVS | CVS Health | ~$55B | Aetna (insurance) + Caremark (PBM) + retail pharmacy + Oak Street clinics | The most consumer-facing; activist pressure to break up; balance sheet stressed. |
| HUM | Humana | ~$30B | MA pure-play (~85% of revenue) | Highest beta to MA rate cycle. Most exposed if rates stay restrictive; most upside if they normalize. |

## Tier 3 — only if relevant

- **HCA Healthcare (HCA)** ~$80B — largest US hospital operator. Useful provider-side comp; benefits when payer MA rates are cut.
- **Tenet Healthcare (THC)** ~$15B — hospital + ambulatory surgery centers.
- **DaVita (DVA)** ~$10B — dialysis specialist; useful for capitated provider economics.
- **Encompass Health (EHC)** ~$10B — post-acute / rehab specialist.
- **Walgreens (WBA)** — private as of late 2025 (Sycamore Partners take-private). Adjacent retail pharmacy comp.

## Recently public / pre-IPO worth watching

- **Counterforce, Aledade-style ACO operators** — smaller MA risk-bearing entities. Not yet public.
- **Various Optum-style services rollups** — most still acquired, not public.

## Notable recent corporate actions

- **Brian Thompson assassination (December 2024)** — CEO of UnitedHealthcare division. Cultural and political catalyst.
- **Andrew Witty resignation as UNH CEO (May 2025).** Stephen Hemsley returned as CEO.
- **Karen Lynch out as CVS CEO (October 2024)**; David Joyner in.
- **Cigna's MA business divested to HCSC (2024).**
- **Walgreens taken private by Sycamore Partners (2025).**
- **Berkshire Hathaway entered UNH position Q2 2025 (revealed Aug 2025); exited entirely by Q1 2026 (revealed in 13F filed May 15, 2026).**
- **Change Healthcare cyberattack (February 2024)** — exposed 100M+ patient records; $4B+ remediation costs at UNH; continued operational and regulatory overhang.
- **DOJ criminal and civil investigations into UNH MA upcoding (revealed 2025)** — ongoing.
