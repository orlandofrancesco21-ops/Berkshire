# Branded athletic footwear & apparel — players

Curated list of names worth understanding. May 2026 market caps approximate.

## Tier 1 — must understand

The names that define the sector.

| Ticker | Company | Market cap | Positioning | Why it matters |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| NKE | Nike | ~$63B | Global #1 incumbent; mid-turnaround | Largest brand in the category. Whatever happens here sets the tape for the whole sector. |
| ADS.DE | Adidas | ~€40B | Global #2; mid-recovery under Gulden | The Yeezy-recovery case study. Currently on the better side of the brand-heat cycle. |
| DECK | Deckers | ~$18B | Owns Hoka (running insurgent) + UGG | The Hoka cash flow is funding the most successful insurgent of the cycle. Tests whether brand-heat in running translates to durable economics. |
| ONON | On Holding | ~$15B | Premium running-into-lifestyle insurgent | Pure-play test of "is brand-heat durable?" The most expensive name in the category. |
| LULU | Lululemon | ~$30B | Premium women's training/athleisure; testing first US slowdown | The category leader in margin. First real cyclical test of the brand. |

## Tier 3 — only if relevant

- **Brooks** — private (owned by Berkshire Hathaway). The pure-play running specialist. Strong but smaller than Hoka.
- **New Balance** — private. Notable for the retro/lifestyle revival 2022-2026.
- **Salomon** — under Amer Sports above; tracking the gorpcore moment.
- **VF Corp (VFC)** — owns Vans, North Face, Timberland. Adjacent (lifestyle/outdoor) but Vans specifically is in deep brand-decline.
- **Columbia Sportswear (COLM)** — outdoor adjacent.

## Recently public / pre-IPO worth watching

- **Vuori** — pre-IPO; the premium men's athleisure challenger to Lululemon. Filed confidentially in 2025; IPO 2026 TBD. Watch as direct comp on Lululemon thesis.
- **Alo Yoga** — private; the other premium women's athleisure player threatening Lululemon at the very top.
- **Salomon brand within Amer Sports** — already public via AS but the brand's solo trajectory is worth segmenting in analysis.

## Recently taken private

- **Skechers** — taken private at ~$63/share by 3G Capital, October 2025. Removed from the public set; relevant as historical comp and as wild card if 3G's cost-cut playbook creates share donations to other names.
