## What's normal vs what's stretched

**Stretched on the cheap side (incumbents):**
- Nike at 18-20x forward P/E, 3.5%+ dividend yield, 6-7% FCF yield — bottom-quintile of its own 15-year valuation history. Discount is real but partially earned (margin compression, China headwind).
- Puma at ~10x P/E — even cheaper but reflects a smaller, less-resilient franchise.

**Stretched on the rich side (insurgents):**
- On Holding at ~50x forward P/E — pricing in continued high-growth durability that has not been tested through a US recession.
- Deckers at ~22x forward P/E — meaningful re-rate lower from 2024 peak of ~30x as Hoka growth decelerates. Still rich if Hoka durability fails.

**Normal:**
- Adidas at ~22-25x P/E on recovering earnings — fair given the trajectory.
- Lululemon at ~18-20x P/E — multiple has compressed from 35x+ as US growth slowed; now closer to fair value than rich.

**The big call:** if the consensus view that "Nike has structurally lost the brand" turns out to be only partially right, current pricing is generous. If it turns out to be largely right, current pricing is barely fair. This is the cycle of opinion within which the next 3-year return is determined.
