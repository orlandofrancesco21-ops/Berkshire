# Branded athletic footwear & apparel — primer

**Created:** 2026-05-20
**Last updated:** 2026-05-20
**Framework reference:** `../../frameworks/branded-athletic-footwear-and-apparel.md`

---

## TL;DR

The sector is mid-cycle from a brand-heat perspective with a clear narrative trade going on: Nike (the global incumbent) is mid-turnaround under a returning insider CEO, Adidas is mid-comeback after near-disaster, Lululemon is testing its first real US slowdown, and the insurgents (On, Hoka via Deckers) are at peak cultural relevance with the durability question still unanswered. Greater China is a structural headwind for the foreign incumbents — the local brand thesis (Anta, Li-Ning) has been validated and is unlikely to fully reverse. Tariffs are the policy wild card — 2025-2026 US tariff escalations affect every name with cost-of-goods exposure to Vietnam/Indonesia/China/Cambodia. The contrarian view worth holding: **the incumbents (Nike specifically) are pricing in permanent share loss to insurgents when historically brand cycles mean-revert on 5-10 year horizons**; the consensus view is that this time is structurally different because of TikTok-mediated brand discovery and Chinese consumer nationalism. Both sides are partly right; the question for any investment is which side the current price embeds.

## Industry structure

See `../../frameworks/branded-athletic-footwear-and-apparel.md` for the general industry-structure treatment. Sector-specific points to flag:

- The wholesale-DTC pendulum has swung back toward wholesale across the entire industry 2024-2026. Every CEO transcript references it. This is partly a Nike-specific correction and partly an industry-wide recognition that the pure-DTC thesis was overrated.
- Tariffs are now structural enough that they belong in mid-cycle margin analysis, not as a one-off bridge item. Most brands have given up on "passing through" beyond modest price increases — consumers are stretched.
- The "athlete endorsement → cultural permission → pricing power" chain is intact, but the cultural carriers have changed. Less LeBron / more TikTok creator-athletes. This is harder for incumbents to navigate than for digital-native insurgents.

## What's changed in the last 5 years

The shifts that matter for forward-looking thesis work:

- **Hoka and On have permanently arrived.** What looked like fashion cycles in 2021 has converted to durable franchises. Hoka is a $2.5B+ brand under Deckers; On is on track for $3B+ revenue. They have taken meaningful run-specialty share from Nike and Brooks and converted it into lifestyle/casual share.
- **Nike's DTC overshoot collapsed publicly.** John Donahoe's "Consumer Direct Acceleration" strategy (2020-2024) over-rotated to DTC, alienated wholesale, and starved the brand of distribution and authenticity. Elliott Hill returned as CEO in October 2024 to reverse it. By 2026 the rebuild is visible in wholesale revenue growth but margin recovery lags.
- **Greater China structurally rolled over for foreign brands.** What looked cyclical in 2022 looks structural by 2026 — Chinese consumers have shifted to local brands (Anta, Li-Ning, Bosideng) for both nationalism and product reasons. Nike Greater China revenue has been in negative growth for ~10 quarters running. Adidas has fared somewhat better but is also below 2019 levels.
- **Tariffs hit the entire industry.** US tariff escalations on China (2018+) and the 2025-2026 expansion to Vietnam/Indonesia have added 200-400 bps of gross margin pressure across the sector. Nike alone called out ~$1.5B FY26 tariff headwind.
- **Lululemon's US slowdown.** After ~15 years of mid-teens revenue growth, US growth turned single-digit in 2024-2025. Whether this is cyclical, brand-fatigue, or competitive (Vuori, Alo, Athleta) is the most-debated question in the category.
- **Adidas survived Yeezy.** The Kanye West/Yeezy partnership unwind in 2022 nearly broke the brand financially. Bjorn Gulden's appointment (Jan 2023) and the Samba/Gazelle terrace-sneaker revival have turned Adidas into a credible turnaround case study.
- **Skechers went private (2025).** 3G Capital LBO'd Skechers at ~$63/share. Removes a public benchmark from the value/mass-market end of the category.

## What's likely to change in the next 5 years

Where consensus might be wrong:

- **Nike turnaround takes longer than the bulls expect but works.** Consensus 2026 is split between "Hill turns it around in 18 months" and "the brand is broken permanently." Base case is a multi-year (3-5y) rebuild with margin recovery in FY28-29 not FY27, but the franchise is intact. Watch sell-through at Foot Locker/Dick's quarterly as the truth-teller.
- **Insurgent durability gets tested in a downturn.** On and Hoka have grown through a benign consumer environment. The first real US consumer recession is the test — do customers stay loyal or do they revert to incumbents (or just buy fewer shoes)?
- **Greater China stays impaired.** The local brand share gains look more permanent than cyclical. Base case: Greater China runs at 50-70% of 2021 peak revenue for the global incumbents indefinitely. The bull case (mean reversion) requires a thesis on Chinese consumer behaviour that's hard to defend.
- **Tariffs persist; manufacturing diversification accelerates.** India, Bangladesh, Cambodia get more share. Vietnam stays dominant. Margin recovery from tariff mitigation is 2-3 years out at best.
- **Marketing budget inflation is permanent.** TikTok-era brand-building requires constant content investment. Nike's $5B FY26 marketing spend is the new floor, not a one-off.
- **Athlete deal escalation in women's basketball and women's running.** Caitlin Clark, Angel Reese, Sha'Carri Richardson era — Nike, Adidas, and Puma are paying up. Watch endorsement spend per revenue dollar.

## Where economic profit pools sit

Most of the economic profit in the value chain sits with **brand owners** (Nike, Adidas, Lululemon, Deckers) when their brands have cultural permission. Operating margins of 12-18% on capital-light models translate to 25-40%+ ROIC. The 2nd-largest profit pool sits with the **specialist wholesale aggregators** (Foot Locker, Dick's, JD Sports) but with operating margins of only 4-8% — the brand owner captures roughly 2-3x more profit per dollar of revenue than the retailer.

Manufacturers (mostly Vietnamese and Indonesian — Pou Chen, Feng Tay, Yue Yuen) operate on 4-7% operating margins. They are critical but not powerful — the brand owner sets specifications, designs, and timing. Manufacturer concentration is high (top 3 make ~60% of Nike's footwear) but interchangeable enough that the brand owner retains pricing power.

The third profit pool — and the one that's grown the most — is in **insurgent brands that achieve cultural moment**. Hoka inside Deckers has gone from a rounding error in 2014 to >40% of Deckers' revenue and a clear majority of its operating profit. On has gone from money-losing pre-IPO (2021) to ~$3B revenue at high-teens operating margin.

## Open questions

- **Is Nike's brand erosion structural or cyclical?** The single biggest question for any NKE position. Frame: has Nike permanently lost the right to charge $180 for an Air Max, or is the current price ($42-50 range) discounting that risk?
- **What's the through-cycle Greater China margin for foreign incumbents?** Not what's the recovery to peak — what's the new normal? Consensus models still assume 30%+ EBIT margin in China; the realised trajectory is sub-20% for several years.
- **How durable are On and Hoka through a real consumer recession?** No data yet — both have grown only in benign macro.
- **Is the wholesale-DTC re-balance permanent, or will the pendulum swing again?** Likely permanent for 5-7 years given the burn marks, but plan a re-evaluation if a new generation of DTC tech (AR fit, hyper-personalized .com, etc.) makes another DTC pivot economically rational.
- **Does Lululemon's male/international segment offset US slowdown?** Long-term thesis on the brand depends on this.
- **What happens to Skechers under 3G ownership?** 3G's playbook (cost cuts, no marketing) is a poor fit for branded consumer. Could create share donations to other players.
